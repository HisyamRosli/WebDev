Employ a product ownership model have the smallest amount of friction as we navigate the seismic shift in talent alignment and workforce enablement. During a product ownership model, teams organize around a product or suite of products and therefore the team contains all the talents and capabilities required to develop and deliver that product end-to-end.

At an equivalent time, this needs a dramatic shift within the way we expect about reporting relationships, product oversight, and talent management. rather than a manager overseeing an employee’s overall performance, work quality, career pathing, skill development, training plans, and more, a product ownership model breaks those responsibilities across product and talent, or practice.

Organisation modernization

Our employees are increasingly using mobile devices for work-related tasks:

Most employees now take their mobile devices to figure and use apps to accomplish work-related tasks. They use cloud-based apps for email, note-taking, file sharing, messaging, and lots of others.

All this translates into a highly vulnerable and potentially compliance-violating practice referred to as shadow IT. By making a deliberate move to the cloud, we will incorporate strict BYOD policies and regulate the utilization of cloud-based apps during a safer, monitored, and centrally-managed environment. So, we have team members performing from home.

Geographically dispersed teams require collaboration tools to figure more efficiently and to finish projects on time. Cloud solutions are perfect for this because they’re inherently equipped with internet connectivity and online collaboration capabilities. By moving to the cloud, we will provide teams access to company-managed tools for web conferencing, file sharing, emailing, instant messaging, etc.
