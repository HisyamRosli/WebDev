What are the most common pitfalls that are to be avoided

According to ziffity, searchcio.techtarget and ciodive:

  • Wasn’t well prepared with the Planning in terms of business and functional impact, possible errors that many occur, lack of comprehension and the wrong way of executing it also not being well verse with the applications. To fix this is, to first understand the goal of the company and align them with the needs of the company, this also needs the staff to be well verse with the systems provided, if you are running a big company you need an it management to counter errors that may occur.
  • BBuilding the cloud to be functional today without proper planning for the future and lack of testing, how you prevent this is by planning properly and having discussing with your migration team
  • Estimate wrongly the performance issues and how much it can cost to fix, how you can prevent this is by estimating it properly and budget properly to counter the problem
  • Not preparing the proper people to operate the migration, in light of this issue, you prepare the proper staff that is need to operate this migration
  • having the thought process of all the cloud service providers the same way, this is the train of thought process as different cloud provider provides different services, and only by doing research and reviews could understand what they are

What are the most common pitfalls that are to be avoided for aws:

According to acloudguru, easydeloy, techcrunch

  • One of the common mistake during cloud migration is not reevaluating the service when changing the business, not matter how easy the process is, when the service is not needed to be used, then you don’t have to use it, without confirming is a big problem when migrating.
  • Bad cost handling, paying for more than needed, executing future planning to early, overlooking things that can cost more even though it’s not need, and it can cause a lot of issues if you are not managing it only waiting for the final bill to come at the end of the month, how you tackle this is by having a proper cost handling, only use what is needed for the end user.
  • Not evenly distributing responsibility with users, providing the users with too many permissions, it could cost your Aws environment to be vulnerable to problems, causing human errors, security issues and configuration problems, how you prevent is by only having a few admins because not all of the user needs to be, and restricting the access needed for their jobs
  • Not giving the end users the proper training and certification needed, it would be easier if the IT staff is speaking a similar language to ensure management of the cloud resources.
  • Auto scaling is not being used, using auto scaling helps you to create scaling polices for using resources across different services, auto scaling also provides you with recommendations on what need to optimize performance and cost also making it balance for the two of them.
